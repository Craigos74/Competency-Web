Regulatory context and standards alignment

Alumet’s competency framework has been developed to support the requirements of the Building Safety Act and aligns with the principles of BS 8670, supported by recognised industry assurance through the Common Assessment Standard.

Our assessments are structured to demonstrate that individuals undertaking façade-related roles are competent for their responsibilities, appropriately supported, operating within defined limits of authority, and equipped to escalate risk or uncertainty where required.

Building Safety Act alignment

We support the Act’s emphasis on competence, accountability and effective risk management by defining role responsibilities, embedding safety-led behaviours, maintaining evidence of assessment and review, and applying clear navigation and escalation routes.

BS 8670 alignment

We assess competence using Skills, Knowledge, Experience and Behaviours (SKEB), applied proportionately to role, responsibility and project risk, supported by evidence and periodic review.

SKEB-based competency assessments

All roles within Alumet are assessed using a SKEB-based framework, aligned to BS 8670 principles. Competency is evaluated across:

Skills

Ability to carry out defined tasks and responsibilities effectively.

Knowledge

Understanding of relevant regulations, standards, systems and interfaces.

Experience

Demonstrated application of skills and knowledge at appropriate complexity and scale.

Behaviours

Application of SAFE MINDSET behaviours in real project environments.

How competence is managed

1. DefineRole requirements set by responsibility and risk.
2. AssessAssessment against SKEB and SAFE MINDSET.
3. EvidenceRecords captured and reviewed.
4. DevelopTargeted actions, mentoring and training.
5. EscalateClear protocols where thresholds are exceeded.

Governance and assurance

Competency assessments are subject to management oversight and periodic review. Where gaps or limitations are identified, appropriate controls are implemented, including supervision, mentoring, training or escalation.

Common Assessment Standard (CAS) – Constructionline

Alumet has achieved the Common Assessment Standard (CAS) through Constructionline, an industry-recognised pre-qualification standard used widely across the UK construction sector.

CAS provides a consistent benchmark for organisational capability, governance and compliance. Many principal contractors and public sector clients specify CAS as a minimum requirement, particularly on larger and higher-risk projects. The current version includes a dedicated Building Safety section, supporting evidence of organisational systems, competence management and governance arrangements in line with Building Safety Act expectations.

  • Independently assessed against a recognised industry benchmark
  • Robust systems and processes to manage safety and competence
  • Alignment with procurement and regulatory expectations
